Tarun

student / social entrepreneur / builder

I’m a senior at Olentangy Liberty High School exploring how technology can be leveraged for social good. On one side, I’ve built a strong technical foundation through computer science, data modeling, and entrepreneurial projects focused on real-world impact. On the other hand, I’ve intentionally developed an interdisciplinary lens across civics, environmental policy, and social innovation so I can better understand the human systems behind the problems I want to solve. Together, these experiences have shaped my interest in social entrepreneurship and building technology that serves people at scale.

Beyond academics, I’m a debate captain and national competitor who loves tackling big ideas and learning how to think, communicate, and lead under pressure. I’m fascinated by startup culture and spend time exploring venture ecosystems, currently spending my second semester diving into Y Combinator’s Spring ’26 prompts and thinking about problems worth building solutions for.

Outside of projects, you’ll usually find me playing pickup basketball or pickleball, developing my public speaking through my temple’s Hindu Dharma program, thrifting, or spending time with my friend group, Las Uvas (“the grapes” in Spanish), on Uvas Fridays. I also enjoy the lighter side of the internet—yes, that includes watching the chaotic influencer boxing events (man, Jake Paul’s actually pretty good).

Looking ahead, I hope to study computer science and business in college while continuing to build ventures that create meaningful impact. If I’m going to spend my life working on something, I want it to be work that has me feeling good, and social entrepreneurship feels like the path where that purpose and my curiosity meet.
