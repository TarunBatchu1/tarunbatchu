Overview

Across my school competition work, my focus hasn’t been just competitive excellence, but on opening new pathways and setting precedents for students who follow, expanding opportunities, launching new initiatives, and creating structures that make it easier for underclassmen to participate and succeed.

Future Business Leaders of America

Founded my school’s FBLA chapter independently after recognizing that many students were unable to participate in DECA due to course-schedule requirements. In the first year, I overcame administrative resistance to launch the chapter, build membership, and mentor competitors—resulting in 11 National Leadership Conference qualifications (100% of competitors). The program has since expanded districtwide and grown into one of the largest FBLA chapters in the state, now serving 135+ members.

Debate

Expanded our program by bridging the traditional divide between Lincoln-Douglas and Public Forum, encouraging cross-event participation and collaboration. As a Lincoln-Douglas debater, I earned the top state tournament record in my city, and also qualified for Public Forum Nationals, where my team placed as the second-highest from Ohio. After nationals, I built relationships with top debaters who now provide guest lectures and training sessions for our team. Beyond our school, I helped grow DebateResources.com to 11.5K+ nonprofit users and facilitated prep exchanges connecting 100+ under-resourced teams through national debate equity servers.

Health Occupations Students of America

As a freshman, I wanted to compete in HOSA’s biomedical debate category after debate season ended—but there were no students involved in both activities. I recruited two students with no prior debate experience and we spent months preparing medical malpractice cases daily after school until we had mastered the legal and clinical landscape. That year, we became state champions and the highest-placing freshman team at the international competition.

Even though I don’t plan to pursue medicine, we recognized HOSA’s value as an opportunity for more students, so we developed a report to the International Leadership Committee advocating for greater accessibility and equity, and we were able to present our ideas to members.
