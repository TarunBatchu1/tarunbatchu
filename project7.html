Innovation Competitions & Technology Ventures

Invention Convention

I started Invention Convention in kindergarten with the ambitious creation of the “Light-Up Seatbelt 2000,” a strip of glow-in-the-dark tape wrapped around a seatbelt latch so my friend could find it in the dark. Come competition time, I was brutally crushed. At that age, it’s easy to gravitate toward what you’re naturally good at and avoid what you’re not. But Invention Convention was different—it was the first time I genuinely enjoyed the journey more than the destination. That experience sparked an 11-year commitment to inventing, from solving everyday frustrations to building technologies aimed at global challenges.

In recent years, much of my work has shifted toward environmental innovation—a space that also shapes my advocacy beyond technology.

One example is Trident, a modular sludge-to-energy system that enhances traditional anaerobic digestion through electrochemical pretreatment, circular reuse of recovered materials, and Bayesian optimization to improve the efficiency and stability of biogas production.

GAYA is a modular ocean-based renewable technology platform designed to simultaneously generate clean energy and remove ocean pollution. The system combines ocean thermal energy conversion (OTEC), vertical circulation/upwelling, and photocatalytic reactors to harness temperature gradients, circulate nutrients, and break down microplastics and pollutants.

Global Youth Entrepreneurship Challenge

With one foot in innovation and the other in entrepreneurship, I lead a team of eight students in the Global Youth Entrepreneurship Challenge, developing Matra—a circular business model that uses AI-driven textile sorting and enzyme chemistry to convert waste into high-value materials like graphene aerogels. Our project was named a national finalist.

National Federation for Teaching Entrepreneurship

After placing 3rd in NFTE’s World Series of Innovation last year with UpLyft, our team returned this year to compete across all six categories, submitting projects aligned with five UN Sustainable Development Goals.

We’ve now advanced past the initial stage and are currently prototyping three of our most promising new projects.
