Starting Local: Powell Youth Council

I began my civic journey on the Powell Youth Council, where I quickly realized that youth voices in local government weren't just underrepresented—they were often completely absent from conversations that directly affected us.

Scaling Impact: Carnegie Young Leaders

Fellowship & Funding

I was selected as a Carnegie Young Leaders Fellow with the Institute for Citizens & Scholars, receiving an $8,000 grant to expand youth civic programming. This recognition validated that the work we were doing locally could have national and even international impact.

Building Networks

Through my fellowship, I partnered with the Youth Advisory Project to connect 50+ youth advisory boards nationwide. The goal was simple: youth councils shouldn't have to reinvent the wheel. By creating a network where we could share strategies, resources, and lessons learned, we could all be more effective.

International Expansion

That work has since expanded internationally to Kenya and the UAE, where we helped establish and support youth civic programming. We’re now taking an even more ambitious step by addressing barriers to civil society and public assembly, including policies like Jordan’s Public Gatherings Law.

Carnegie Young Leaders Video

National Impact: Citizens & Scholars YAC

Most recently, I was selected as one of six national Youth Advisory Council members from a pool of 250 applicants for the 2026–2028 term. My current initiative focuses on mapping civic engagement deserts—using data analysis to identify communities that lack accessible entry points into civic participation. This work will inform a $7M funding and policy proposal to expand civic access and participation.
